CODE OF CONDUCT
===============

1. Professional Behavior
   All employees are expected to conduct themselves in a professional manner at all times.
   This includes treating colleagues, clients, and partners with respect and courtesy.
   Harassment, discrimination, bullying, or intimidation of any kind will not be tolerated
   and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

2. Equal Opportunity
   The company is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate based on race, color,
   religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, sexual orientation,
   gender identity, veteran status, or any other protected characteristic. All employment
   decisions are based on merit, qualifications, and business needs.

3. Anti-Harassment Policy
   Harassment includes any unwelcome conduct based on a protected characteristic that creates
   an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment. This includes but is not limited to:
   - Verbal harassment (slurs, jokes, comments, threats)
   - Physical harassment (unwanted touching, blocking movement)
   - Visual harassment (offensive images, gestures, emails)
   - Sexual harassment (unwanted advances, requests for favors)
   Employees who experience or witness harassment should report it immediately to their
   manager, HR, or through the anonymous ethics hotline at 1-800-555-ETHICS.

4. Conflict of Interest
   Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict or appear to conflict
   with company interests. This includes outside employment, financial interests in
   competitors or suppliers, and personal relationships that may influence business decisions.
   All potential conflicts must be disclosed to the employee's manager and HR in writing.

5. Confidentiality
   Employees must protect confidential company information, trade secrets, and proprietary
   data. This obligation continues after employment ends. Confidential information includes
   but is not limited to: financial data, customer lists, business strategies, product plans,
   employee records, and any information marked as confidential.

6. Use of Company Resources
   Company resources (equipment, software, email, internet) are provided for business use.
   Limited personal use is permitted as long as it does not interfere with work duties,
   does not violate any policies, and does not consume excessive resources. Employees should
   have no expectation of privacy when using company-provided resources.

7. Social Media Policy
   Employees are free to use social media on personal time. However, when posting content
   that could be associated with the company, employees must:
   - Clearly state that views are their own and not the company's
   - Not disclose confidential company information
   - Not make disparaging comments about the company, colleagues, or clients
   - Comply with all other company policies

8. Reporting Violations
   Employees have a duty to report any violations of this Code of Conduct, company policies,
   or applicable laws. Reports can be made to:
   - Direct manager
   - Human Resources department
   - Anonymous ethics hotline: 1-800-555-ETHICS
   - Ethics email: ethics@company.com
   The company prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports a violation in good faith.

9. Disciplinary Process
   Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in disciplinary action, which may include:
   - Verbal warning
   - Written warning
   - Suspension without pay
   - Termination of employment
   The severity of the action will depend on the nature and circumstances of the violation.
   Serious violations may result in immediate termination without prior warnings.

10. Acknowledgment
    All employees are required to read, understand, and acknowledge this Code of Conduct
    upon hire and annually thereafter. Questions about any provision should be directed to
    the Human Resources department.
